Your original framing has two steps, and neither of them is optimization:
Step 1 — Prediction (forward). Macroclimate + farm design → microclimate. This is a regression/prediction problem. You give it one set of inputs, it returns one predicted microclimate. No searching, no "best" — just predict.

So where did "optimization" come from? It's a third, optional layer that sits on top and only appears when you flip the question around:

Forward question (what you described): "I have this design — what microclimate, and is the crop happy?" → prediction + scoring.

Optimization is literally just running your predict-then-score pipeline hundreds of times in a loop and picking the winner. It reuses Steps 1 and 2 — it doesn't replace them.
