2 · Crop suitability (limiting-factor scored)

Score = the worst-matched variable (Liebig's law of the minimum). The limiting factor is named so you know what to fix. Overall confidence = lowest confidence among the variables actually constraining the score.

Methodology, coefficients & confidence — read before trusting numbers

Every layer below is implemented in this page. Coefficients are literature priors, not locally calibrated — they encode the right physics and direction, with magnitudes to be tightened by your TMS-4 sensors next year. This is the "sound methodology now, cross-verify later" design you asked for.

Layer 2 — Suitability scoring

Each predicted variable gets a trapezoidal membership (1 inside the crop's ideal band, ramping to 0 at its tolerance limits) against the envelopes from the reachability matrix. Crop score = 100 × min(memberships) — the limiting factor governs, never an average. Wind is one-sided (lower better, scaled by crop sensitivity).

Layer 3 — Inverse design

A grid search (this page) over overstorey × LAI (0.5–3.0) × windbreak height × porosity evaluates Layers 1–2 for every design and returns the highest-scoring one. In the research build this becomes Bayesian optimisation / NSGA-II — same objective, smarter search.

Honest limits. (1) Coefficients are not locally validated — treat absolute numbers as indicative, comparisons between designs as more reliable than any single value. (2) Humidity/VPD is weak without sensors. (3) Suitability = "can grow," not yet "profitable/reliable." All three resolve with the year-2 sensor campaign.

Sources: De Frenne et al. 2019/2021 (canopy buffering); Beer–Lambert k (Forest Science); shelterbelt porosity (UMCA); crop envelopes (FAO ECOCROP / extension). See data_source_catalog.html.
