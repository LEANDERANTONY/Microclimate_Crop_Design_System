Crop × Canopy Reachability Matrix

First-pass screen: which high-value crops are physically reachable under which canopy system, judged on the levers you can actually control — shade/light and wind — before any modeling. v1 · June 2026

How to read this. Temperature, humidity and rainfall here are treated as the macroclimate baseline your site already provides — a coconut-suitable tropical site (≈25–35 °C, 2000–3000 mm, RH 70–90%) comfortably covers the temperature/rainfall/humidity bands of all five crops. So the deciding question is not "is the climate right" but "can the canopy + windbreak design bend light and wind into each crop's required window." That is exactly the controllable-variable problem you defined. Soil moisture is assumed regulated by bores and is excluded as a driver.

What this first screen already tells you. Coconut (wide, mature) and silver oak are the two overstoreys that keep every candidate crop in-window — they're the safe structural backbone. Mahogany over-shades the light-sensitive crops, and teak is the clear loser for shade-dependent intercrops because its dry-season leaf drop exposes them at the worst moment. The three wind-sensitive crops (vanilla, cocoa, nutmeg) all flip to viable only with a perimeter windbreak — confirming windbreak design is a first-class decision, not an afterthought. Vanilla is the fussiest (narrow steady-shade band); ginger is the most forgiving fallback.
