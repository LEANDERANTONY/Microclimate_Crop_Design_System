Data Source Catalog — Microclimate Predictor (Step 1)

Extensive sourcing for "macroclimate + farm structure → microclimate." Cast wide now; feature-engineer and prune later. v1 · June 2026

How the columns map to the model. Inputs (green) are predictors the model reads. Targets (orange) are the microclimate labels the model learns to reproduce — the scarce, valuable part. Ancillary (olive) are modifiers (soil, terrain) that condition the offset. Almost everything here is free and pullable through Google Earth Engine in one place; the few paid/effort items are flagged. Resolutions span 1 m → 30 km on purpose — you downscale/aggregate to a common grid during feature engineering.

Feature-engineering reminders for later (don't prune yet). From these raw layers you'll derive a lot: temporal stats (monthly/seasonal min/mean/max, growing-degree-days, dry-spell length), diurnal range from day/night LST, VPD from temp+RH, canopy-structure composites (LAI × height × cover interaction terms — the physical drivers of buffering), texture/structure from Sentinel-1, and topographic indices. The model predicts the offset (sub-canopy − ambient), so always pair every canopy feature with the matching ambient macroclimate value.
