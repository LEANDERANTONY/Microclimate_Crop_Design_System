Growth envelope · disease drivers · variety susceptibility · India demand · local suitability verdict. Anchored to Pattukkottai (Thanjavur): ~28 °C mean (40 °C May, 20 °C Jan nights), RH ~70–73%, ~900–1000 mm rain concentrated in the NE monsoon (Oct–Dec), hot dry summers, alluvial/clay delta soils. v1 · June 2026

The point of this list. Crops span a spectrum: some you can plant directly, some are marginal-but-engineerable (the whole thesis — microclimate/variety/timing tips them into viability), and a few are honestly not worth fighting your climate for. The disease column is deliberately prominent because, as we established, disease — not raw climate — is often what decides profitability here, and it's driven by the same microclimate variables you can engineer. The key local lever is that your humid risk window is the NE monsoon (Oct–Dec) while summers are hot/dry — so flowering/fruiting timing (bahar) is as powerful as canopy or windbreaks.

What this catalog tells you for modelling. Three groups emerge. (1) Direct fruits (guava, sapota, custard apple, mango, moringa, amla, lime, banana, papaya, fig) — robust to your climate; modelling mostly adds disease-timing value. (2) Engineerable (pomegranate, dragon fruit, grapes, ginger, pepper) — these are where your system earns its keep: e.g. dragon fruit needs light summer shade against >38°C sunburn (a canopy/shade-net design problem), and pomegranate/grapes live or die by whether fruiting is kept out of the humid NE-monsoon window (a timing + airflow problem). (3) Heavy/Avoid (vanilla, cocoa, nutmeg; avocado) — either demand year-round shade+humidity your semi-arid site lacks, or need an altitude you don't have. Start the serious modelling on group 2 — maximum decision value, clearest microclimate levers.
